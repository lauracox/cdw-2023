The Fifth

Charlottesville Design Week

April 9—13, 2024

Charlottesville is many things to many people: Jefferson-town, home of the Hoos, the nation’s fastest-growing tech hub, and now? Virginia’s design destination.

For three years, hundreds of the commonwealth’s designers have convened in Cville for a week of connecting, learning, and sharing. The Charlottesville Design Week is completely volunteer-led and has two simple goals:

  1. Develop a strong design community in central Virginia
  2. Convince the general public that good design is worth a damn

Whether you push pixels, press ink, set type, build environments, or do anything else design-related, you will feel right at home during our growing festival.

Get insight into its AIA award-winning design on this tour, led by architects from the design team of Wolf Ackerman and EskewDumezRipple along with the project’s landscape architect, Gregg Bleam.

Thursday

11th

  • Gropen Tour

    Thursday, April 11 from 10:00 – 11:00am at Gropen

    Take a tour of the offices and fabrication shop where graphics are made 3-D. Gropen designs and builds the beautiful signage you see around town, as well as exhibits for the Smithsonian and Monticello, and environmental graphics for architectural projects nationwide. You’ll see all the high and low tech methods they employ, such as water jet cutting, wood and metal working, and flat bed direct printing.

    Thursday, April 11 from 12:30 – 5:00pm around Downtown
